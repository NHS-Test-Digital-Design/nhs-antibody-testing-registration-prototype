About twice-weekly testing for coronavirus (COVID-19)

Dear {{ data['first-name'] or 'John' }} {{ data['last-name'] or 'Smith' }}

Your test pack for twice weekly testing should have arrived.

If not, see the tracking email sent to you by Royal Mail, or call the contact centre (details below).

Some important reminders about below.

Thank you for helping to stop the spread of the virus.

1). Take a test twice a week

It's best if your household (or bubble) all try to do this on the same days 3 or 4 days apart).

To avoid an invalid (void) result:

  • always read the instructions before you start
  • watch the video on how to take the test
  • get the instructions in other languages and formats (easy read / large print)

Go to GOV.UK: www.gov.uk/rapid-lateral-flow tests

2). Report every result

Report every result (whatever it is) to the NHS on the day you take the test.

It will contain web links and support to help you.

Do this to help the NHS reduce infection rates and stop the spread of the virus.

To save time, report online and use your NHS Login (set one up the first time you report if you need to).

To report a result you will need the schools' unique organisation number. Get this from the school (it's 8 digits).

Go to GOV.UK: www.gov.uk/report-covid19-result

3). Order more tests

Your test pack contains 7 tests.

Order again before you take your last test.

There are 2 ways to do this:

  • collect from a test site - get it the same day
  • order online - get it within 2 days

Go to GOV.UK: www.gov.uk/rapid-lateral-flow tests

Order for your own household (not for the bubble).

4). Stop twice weekly testing if...

If anyone tests positive or gets coronavirus symptoms, tell the school and:

  • stop using the tests
  • self-isolate immediately
  • get a different NHS test to confirm the result

Get NHS help and advice: www.nhs.uk/coronavirus

Contact centre

If you have any questions or problems call the contact centre.

Call 119 in England, Wales and Northern Ireland
Free from mobiles and landlines

Call 0300 303 2713 in Scotland
Charged at your standard network rate

Lines open 7am to 11pm
