You can sign up for a free coronavirus (COVID-19) antibody test to find out if you’ve had the virus before. There is a different test to check if you currently have the virus (sometimes called a “swab test”).

Check you're eligible

If you live in England, you can get an antibody test if:

  • you work in social care
  • you are 18 or over
  • you cannot get to an NHS test site to take the test

Read the guidance on home test kits and who can get them

How to find your nearest antibody NHS test site

You can do the antibody test even if you think you’ve not had the virus before.

About the test

It is a ‘fingerprick’ blood test. It is posted to you and comes with everything you need to take the test and post your sample to get your results

The test checks for coronavirus antibodies. If you have them, it’s likely you’ve had the virus before.

Having coronavirus antibodies does not mean you are guaranteed to be immune (protected) from further infection. You might get the virus again or spread it.

How long it takes to sign up

It should take 5 to 10 minutes to sign up.

After you’ve signed up

You need to wait for a message inviting you to order a test. It’s sent within a few days by email or text. But it can take up to 3 months because test kits are limited and need to be prioritised.

Once you get your invite message, you can order your test.

Why take the test

It will help the NHS and research scientists.

They need to learn more about:

  • who already has the virus
  • how it has spread across the UK
