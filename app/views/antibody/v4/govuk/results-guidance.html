If you’ve had a coronavirus (COVID-19) antibody test there are 3 types of result you can get:

  • positive
  • negative
  • void

You should get your result within 3 to 7 days of taking the test (usually by text message). If you do not get your result, call the coronavirus testing contact centre.

Types of antibody test result

Positive antibody test result

A positive result means the test did find COVID-19 coronavirus antibodies so it is likely you’ve had the virus before. It is not possible to say when.

It does not mean you are guaranteed to be immune (protected) from further infection. You might get the virus again or spread it.

Negative antibody test result

A negative result means the test did not find COVID-19 coronavirus antibodies so it is unlikely you’ve had the virus before at the time you took the test.

Void antibody test result

A void result means the test did not work.

There are many reasons for this and it’s not possible to tell you the exact one. For example, it could be a problem with the test kit, how the test was carried out (like the blood sample collected was too small) or there was a delay between taking the sample and the lab checking it.

A void result is still important for research purposes.

What an antibody test cannot tell you

An antibody test result cannot tell you:

  • if you’re immune to coronavirus
  • if you can or cannot spread the virus to other people
  • if you have the virus at the time you take the test (a “current infection”)

To find out if you have the virus now, there is a different test (sometimes known as a swab or virus test).

An antibody test is different to the swab test to check if you have coronavirus now.

Whatever your test result, you must continue to follow the same guidelines as everyone else to protect yourself and others from the virus.

If you think your result is wrong

The test is accurate but no at-home test is 100% reliable all of the time.

COVID-19 is new and more research needs to be done to understand how our bodies respond to it. For example, if antibodies are always produced and how long they last.

If you know you’ve had the virus before, it’s still possible to get a negative antibody result. This can happen for some people because they do not develop antibodies, or their level of antibodies is too low for the test to detect.

If you think you have not had the virus, it’s still possible to get a positive antibody result. This might happen even if you didn’t have symptoms or only very mild ones as asymptomatic COVID-19 is a known phenomenon.

If you have any concerns about your test result, get advice from a health professional.

Support if you feel anxious or worried about your result

Coronavirus and your well-being

Protect yourself and other from the virus

Regardless of your test result you must continue to follow the same guidelines as everyone else to protect yourself and others from the virus. For example:

  • wash your hands often
  • follow social distancing advice/li>
  • follow any local lockdown restrictions in your area
  • self-isolate and get tested if you (or someone you live with) get symptoms, or you are advised to by NHS Test and Trace
