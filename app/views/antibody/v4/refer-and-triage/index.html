You can sign up for a free coronavirus (COVID-19) antibody test if you work in social care.

You can do this test even if you believe you have not had the virus before.

About the test

The test checks for coronavirus antibodies. If you have them, it’s likely you’ve had the virus before.

This test is a ‘fingerprick’ blood test and:

  • is posted to you so you can do it at home
  • comes with everything you need and simple step-by-step instructions

You take the test and return your sample in the post.

Even if you have coronavirus antibodies, you might not be immune from catching the virus again or spreading it.

How long it takes

It should take 5 to 10 minutes to sign up.

After you’ve signed up

You'll need to wait for a text message. You may get this text message in a few days but it can take up to 3 months.

Once you've received the text message, you'll need to provide some more details and order your test. You'll get it within 5 days.

Once you've taken your test and sent it back, you should get your result within 5 working days.

Why take the test

Your details will help the NHS and research scientists.

They need to learn more about:

  • who has already had the virus
  • how it spreads
