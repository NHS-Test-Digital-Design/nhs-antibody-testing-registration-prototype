You can sign up for a free coronavirus (COVID-19) antibody test if you:

  • work in social care
  • are 18 or over
  • live in England
  • cannot get to an NHS test site to take the test

Read the guidance on who can get the test and how to find your nearest antibody NHS test site

You can do the antibody test even if you think you’ve not had the virus before.

About the test

The test checks for coronavirus antibodies. If you have them, it’s likely you’ve had the virus before.

It is a ‘fingerprick’ blood test. It is posted to you and comes with everything you need to take the test and post your sample to get your results

Even if you have coronavirus antibodies, you might not be immune from catching the virus again or spreading it.

How long it takes to sign up

It should take 5 to 10 minutes to sign up.

After you’ve signed up

You’ll need to wait for an email confirming you have registered. You may get this email in a few days but it can take up to 3 months.

Once you’ve received the email, you can order your test.

Once ordered, it takes 5 days to arrive and about another 5 days to get your results.

Why take the test

It will help the NHS and research scientists.

They need to learn more about:

  • who already has the virus
  • how it has spread across the UK
