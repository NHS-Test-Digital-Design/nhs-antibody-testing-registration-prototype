You can sign up for a free coronavirus (COVID-19) antibody test to find out if you've had the virus before. There is a different test to check if you currently have the virus (sometimes called a “swab test”).

This service is for people in England, Wales, Scotland and Northern Ireland.

Check you're eligible

If you live in England and Wales you can get an antibody test if:

  • you're 18 or over
  • you're a care worker
  • you cannot get to an NHS test site to take the test

If you live in Northern Ireland, you can get an antibody test if:

  • you're 18 or over
  • you're working in primary care (including eye doctors), a pharmacist or a care worker (in care homes or domiciliary care)

If you live in Scotland, you can get an antibody test if:

  • you're 18 or over
  • you work in education with children or young people (teaching or non-teaching roles)
  • you work in early years education or in a primary, secondary or special school

About the test

It is a "fingerprick" blood test done at home. It is posted to you and comes with everything you need. You take the test and post your sample to get your results.

The test checks for coronavirus antibodies. If you have them, it’s likely you’ve had the virus before.

Even if you have coronavirus antibodies it does not mean you are guaranteed to be immune (protected) from further infection. You might get the virus again or spread it.

How long it takes to sign up

It should take 5 to 10 minutes to sign up.

After you've signed up

You need to wait for a message inviting you to order a test. It’s sent within a few days by email or text. But it can take up to 3 months because test kits are limited and need to be prioritised. In Scotland, not everyone who signs up will get a test.

Once you get your invite message, you can order your test.

Once ordered, it takes up to 5 days to arrive and about 5 days to get your results.

Why take the test

It will help the NHS and research scientists. They need to learn more about:

  • who has already had the virus
  • how it has spread in the UK
