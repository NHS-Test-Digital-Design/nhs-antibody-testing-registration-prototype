An antibody test is a blood test to check if you've had coronavirus (COVID-19) before.

You can check if you're eligible to get a test kit.

It's different from the test to check if you have coronavirus now.

About the antibody test kit

The test is a 'fingerprick' blood test which checks for coronavirus antibodies.

The test kit is posted to you. It comes with everything you need. You take the test and post your sample back. Then you'll get your results.

This service is only for checking eligibility for an antibody test kit by post. You cannot use it to book a test at an NHS test site.

What the antibody test can tell you

An antibody test can tell you if it's likely you've had coronavirus before.

An antibody test does not tell you:

  • if you're protected from coronavirus
  • if you can or cannot spread it to other people

Why take the test

Taking the test helps the NHS and research scientists learn more about who's had the virus and how it has spread.

If you've had the vaccine

If you've had the vaccine you are still able to check your eligibility to take an antibody test. This will help the NHS and research scientists to better understand how effective the vaccine is.

Who can apply for a home test kit

The rules for who can apply for an antibody test depend on:

  • if you're comfortable doing a fingerprick blood test
  • if you have a mobile that can recieve text messages
  • if you're 18 or over
  • if you're part of a survey or study
  • where you live
  • your job

Check your eligibility based on your country

England

You can only apply for a test kit if you work in paid adult social care

Wales

You can only apply for a test kit if you work in paid domiciliary care (caring for people in their homes)

Northern Ireland

You can apply for a test kit if you are:

  • working in primary care (for example a GP, pharmacist, community nurse, dental nurse or optician)
  • doing any job in a care home
  • a careworker in paid domiciliary care (caring for people in their homes)

Scotland

You can apply for a test kit if you are:

  • working in education (teaching or non-teaching staff)
  • based at an early years education, primary, secondary or an additional support for learning school setting
  • a careworker in paid domiciliary care (caring for people in their homes)

If you got an invitation email, you can join Public Health Scotland's coronavirus survey. This will make sure your test result is as helpful as possible to Scotland's response to coronavirus. Check your invitation email for the key code to join the survey.

To request an antibody test, follow these steps

1. Confirm your eligibility
1. Enter personal details
3. Wait for your invite and order a test kit
4. Do your test and send it back to get your results
Start now

Once you have confirmed your eligibility and entered your personal details, you'll get an email or text message inviting you to order a test kit. This can take up to 3 weeks.

It should take 5 to 10 minutes to check if you're eligible and enter your personal details.
