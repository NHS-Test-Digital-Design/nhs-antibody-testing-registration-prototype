An antibody test checks to see if you’ve had the virus before.

About self-test kits

Self-test kits are fingerprick blood tests, they:

  • are free and take 5 days to arrive in the post
  • are approved by the NHS as reliable and accurate
  • come with everything you need to test yourself at home and get your result in minutes

With a self-test kit, you do not need to post a blood sample to get a result.

To complete the abc 19 rapid test accurately watch this video to the end and then refer to the instruction sheet.

There are five steps to follow to help you complete the test correctly.

Before you start check you have all parts of the kit within easy reach.

Also gather the additional materials required prepare by washing your hands with nothing but soap and warm water.

Remove the test from the foil pouch choose your middle or ring finger then uncap the lancet and apply gentle pressure on the area highlighted until you hear a click.

You need to wait for a good size drop of blood to form if it doesn't squeeze like this.

Keeping the blood collector at a slight angle gently touch the drop of blood to draw it up to the black line but no further this will happen automatically.

Do not squeeze the bulb continue to the next stage quickly to stop the blood clotting.

Bring the tip of the blood collector to the center of the sample hole.

Carefully squeeze the bulb to help the blood flow.

Reach for the test solution twist open and add all of the liquid to the sample hole.

Drop by drop 20 minutes later your results will be ready.

Review the results to see if the test is positive or negative.

And that's it five steps prepare sample collect run test.

Then check the results you're now ready to use the test.

Remember to refer to the instruction sheet provided when performing the test.
