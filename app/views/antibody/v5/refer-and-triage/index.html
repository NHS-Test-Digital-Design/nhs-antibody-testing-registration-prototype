You can sign up for a free coronavirus (COVID-19) antibody test to find out if you've had the virus before.

There is a different test to check if you currently have the virus.

About the test

The test checks for coronavirus antibodies.

It's a 'fingerprick' blood test done at home. It's posted to you and comes with everything you need.

You take the test and post your sample to get your results.

If the test shows you have coronavirus antibodies

Having coronavirus antibodies means it's likely you've had the virus before.

Even if you have coronavirus antibodies, you might not be immune (protected) from catching the virus again or spreading it.

Eligibility

The rules for getting a test depend on:

  • where you live (England, Wales, Northern Ireland or Scotland)
  • your job

England or Wales

If you live in England or Wales, you can get an antibody test if you're 18 or over and both of these apply:

  • you do any job in a care home
  • you cannot get to an NHS test site to take the test

Scotland

In Scotland, antibody testing is used to help understand levels of previous COVID-19 infection in the population.

If you live in Scotland, you can get a test if you are:

  • 18 or over
  • working in education (both teaching and non-teaching staff)
  • based in an early years education, primary, secondary or special school setting

You may be invited to participate in testing again in the future. However, you should also be aware that signing up does not guarantee that you will receive a test, as we will be looking to select a representative sample.

Northern Ireland

If you live in Northern Ireland, you can sign up for a test as part of a study to understand the spread of coronavirus.

You must be 18 or over and one of these must apply:

  • you work in primary care (for example as a GP, pharmacist, dental nurse or optician)
  • you do any job in a care home
  • you're a care worker and your job involves caring for someone at their home (domiciliary care)
Start now

What you need to know

It should take 5 to 10 minutes to sign up.

After you've signed up

You'll need to wait for a text message or email inviting you to order a test. You may get this invitation in a few days but it can take up to 3 months. In Scotland and Northern Ireland, not everyone who signs up will get a test.

Why take the test

It will help the NHS and research scientists.

They need to learn more about:

  • who has already had the virus
  • how it has spread
