Use this service to register up to 100 COVID-19 test kits for your organisation at once.

Care homes can also use this service to report the results of rapid lateral flow tests (positive, negative or void).

What you'll need

  • your 8 digit unique organisation number (UON)
  • the barcode for each test
  • an email address (and ideally mobile number) to get results

Before you start

  • book a courier collection
  • take the tests on the same day your courier comes to collect them
  • make sure people getting tested have given you consent to register them and to get their results sent to the contact details you enter

What type of tests can you register?

All organisations can use this service to register polymerase chain reaction (PCR) tests, which are swab tests sent to a lab.

Care homes, schools and prisons can also use this service to register rapid lateral flow test kits, which give results in 30 minutes.

Read the COVID-19 online guidance and your printed instructions to learn more about who is eligible for this service and which types of tests you can register.

Which organisations can report a result?

If your organisation is a care home, you can also use this service to report the results of rapid lateral flow tests if you have them.

All other organisations must report their rapid lateral flow test results through the self-report service.

How to use the spreadsheet

Using the correct spreadsheet will save you time if you are registering lots of tests at once. If you do not have the right spreadsheet, you can download it during the registration process.

Make sure you:

  • only record up to 100 test kits per spreadsheet
  • record the details of staff and non-staff on separate spreadsheets
  • remove the details of anyone who isn’t being tested

How we use personal data

To find out how we use people’s data when you register them for a coronavirus test, read the coronavirus privacy note.
