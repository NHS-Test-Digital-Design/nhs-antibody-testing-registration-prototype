Accessibility requirements

Government accessibility requirements

Meet WCAG 2.1 AA guidelines

The service meets level AA of the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 as a minimum.

  • Met

    An audit has taken place and any issues have been fixed.

  • In progress

    An audit is in progress or has been completed but issues have not yet been fixed.

  • Not met

    No audit has taken place.

Have an accessibility statement

The service has published an accessibility statement that follows the standard set out by government.

  • Met

    This has been completed and there is an accessibility statement in place.

  • In progress

    A partial accessibility statement has been completed or it is being worked towards.

  • Not met

    There is no accessibility statement in place or there is an inaccurate statement.

Test with assistive technology

The service must be tested with commonly used assistive technologies and any issues fixed. These include screen magnifiers, screen readers and speech recognition tools.

  • Met

    Testing has taken place with all of the commonly used assistive technologies and any issues fixed.

  • In progress

    Testing has taken place with some of the commonly used assistive technologies but not all of them. Or testing has taken place but the issues have not been fixed.

  • Not met

    No testing has taken place or unknown technologies have been used.

Include people with access needs in research

The service needs to do user research with people who have a range of access needs.

  • Met

    There is evidence that people with access needs have been included in research.

  • In progress

    Some research has taken place with people who have access needs.

  • Not met

    No user research has taken place or it has not included people who have access needs.

Inclusion requirements

End-to-end usability testing

The live service is regularly tested from beginning to end to see how usable it is.

  • Met

    The team does regular end-to-end usability testing with a range of users.

  • In progress

    Some end-to-end usability testing has been done with users, but this is not regular practice.

  • Not met

    No end-to-end usability testing has been completed.

Exclusion mapping

An exclusion mapping workshop has taken place to identify inclusion needs. The team needs to work with the Inclusive Design Team in CC&I (UKHSA) to arrange this.

  • Met

    An exclusion mapping workshop has taken place and any issues have been added to the backlog and prioritised by the team.

  • In progress

    An exclusion mapping workshop is planned or has taken place but issues have not been added to the backlog and prioritised by the team.

  • Not met

    An exclusion mapping workshop has not taken place.
