When you're doing a coronavirus (COVID-19) test, you need to register the test kit so you can get your result.

There are different ways to register a COVID-19 test kit. The register service you need to use depends on why you're doing the test, and how you got the test kit. At some test sites, staff will register your kit for you.

Registering a test kit is different to reporting a test result. You need to report your test result if you're using a rapid lateral flow self-test kit at home or at work. Report a COVID-19 rapid lateral flow test result on GOV.UK

Check which service you should use

This service is for people who have ordered a home PCR test kit and who will return it by post.

If surge testing is happening in your area, you need to register your PCR test kit if you're taking part in surge testing.

If you are arriving in the UK after international travel, you need to register your PCR travel test kit.

If you are taking a PCR test before your hospital visit, you need to register your PCR test kit before a hospital visit.

Check what you need

You should only register a home test kit after you've:

  • received it
  • found your nearest Royal Mail priority postbox or booked a courier to collect it
  • got your order confirmation email containing your bold 10 character order ID (you received this when you ordered your test kit)

If people you live with have received a test kit

  • you should register their test kit too
  • each person must use the test kit they have registered to receive the correct results

Get help to register the test

To have a live video call with trained staff, download the free Be My Eyes app for iOS or for Android and go to the 'specialised help' section.

How we use your data

To find out how we use your personal data when you register for a coronavirus test, read the coronavirus privacy note.
