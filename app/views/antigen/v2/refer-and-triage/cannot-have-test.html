This service is only for people who need a PCR test. Polymerase chain reaction (PCR) tests are mainly for people who have symptoms. They're sent to a lab to be checked.

PCR tests are restricted because there's a limited number available right now.

You will have arrived here because your answers told us you are not eligible for this type of test, or because your symptoms started too long ago.

Get advice on what to do next

If you do not have symptoms, you can take a rapid lateral flow test to check if you’re infectious. Find out if your area offers rapid lateral flow test sites.

Here’s some more advice if you’re in one of the following situations.

I’ve been notified by NHS Test and Trace that I’m a contact of someone who has tested positive for coronavirus but I do not have symptoms

You need to self-isolate immediately for 10 days.

If you live in Scotland or Northern Ireland, you may be asked by contact tracers to get a test. Otherwise, you do not need to get a test unless you go on to get symptoms.

I'm planning to visit someone at higher risk from coronavirus and want to make sure I do not have the virus

You could take a rapid lateral flow test to check if you’re infectious. However, you could still have the virus after a negative result - no test is 100% accurate.

Being vaccinated does not mean you cannot pass the virus on either.

The best thing you can do is to:

  • keep your distance from this person
  • wash your hands regularly
  • use a face covering

If you are visiting someone in a care home, you may be offered a test by the care home.

Find out more about social distancing: what you need to do.

I need to get a test to travel to another country

If your travel company has asked you to get tested, and you do not have symptoms, you can pay for a private test.

You cannot use this free service to get your test.
